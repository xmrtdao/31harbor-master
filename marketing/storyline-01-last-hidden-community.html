For decades, a small waterfront community on a narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and Gardiners Bay has existed largely outside public view. Ownership changed hands quietly. Families passed properties between generations. Residents protected a way of life.

That community is Napeague Camping Club. And for what may be the first time in its history, a residence within this private enclave is being offered to the public market.

The Story

"Like a piece of Nova Scotia tucked in a back corner of Napeague."

This is how locals describe Lazy Point — a place that escaped the luxury-development wave that transformed the rest of the Hamptons. Where baymen and fishermen once lived. Where the population numbers only a few hundred. Where the old Hamptons culture still survives.

What Makes This Different

This is not a mansion. It is not a hedge-fund retreat. It is something far rarer: an authentic piece of coastal history that has somehow remained hidden from the broader market for more than seven decades.

The Napeague Camping Club traces its roots to a 1949 campground. It was incorporated as a not-for-profit community in 1964. For generations, it has been a place apart — a community that intentionally remained different from the rest of the Hamptons.

That story cannot be replicated. It cannot be manufactured. It is real. And it is what makes 31 Harbor Road unlike any other offering on the market today.
