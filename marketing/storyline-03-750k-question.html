In the Hamptons real estate market, $750,000 typically buys a studio apartment or a fixer-upper far from the water. At 31 Harbor Road, it buys something entirely different: a three-bedroom waterfront home in one of the most private communities on the East End.

How is this possible? The answer lies in the unique structure of the Napeague Camping Club — a not-for-profit community where the land is leased from the East Hampton Town Trustees under 35-year leases (renewed in 2019), and the value is driven not by square footage but by location, lifestyle, scarcity, and community access.

The Market Context

Amagansett median home price: $2M–$4M

What You Actually Get

Three bedrooms. Two baths. A deck with water views. Private beach access to Gardiners Bay. A community fee of $175/month. Open year-round — not a seasonal rental. Located 15 minutes from both East Hampton and Montauk.

But more importantly, you get access to a way of life that has all but disappeared from the Hamptons. A community of only a few hundred people. A place where the old maritime culture still survives. A home that sits on a narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and Gardiners Bay — one of the least populated and most beautiful parts of the East End.
