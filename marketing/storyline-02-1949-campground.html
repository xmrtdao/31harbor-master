In 1949, a tent-and-trailer site opened on a quiet stretch of coastline between East Hampton and Montauk. It was not intended to become a real estate phenomenon. It was simply a place where people could camp by the water.

Over the decades, that campground evolved into something unexpected: the Napeague Camping Club — a not-for-profit community incorporated in 1964 that has remained one of the most private and least understood residential enclaves in the Hamptons.

The Timeline

1800s — Lazy Point becomes home to workers from the Smith Meal Fish Factory, one of the major economic engines of the East End. Baymen, fishermen, and seasonal cottagers build a working waterfront community.

1949 — A tent-and-trailer campground is established on the site that will become Napeague Camping Club.

1964 — The club is incorporated as a New York not-for-profit corporation. The community formalizes its unique ownership structure.

2026 — For what may be the first time in the club's history, a residence is offered to the public market. 31 Harbor Road becomes the benchmark for every future transaction.

Why This Matters

This is not merely a property transaction. It is potentially the creation of a public market where one previously did not exist. Questions that this sale may answer:

• What is the market value of a residence within the club?

• Who is the buyer demographic for this unique asset class?

• How should these properties be marketed and financed?

• What level of demand exists outside the community?

The buyer of 31 Harbor Road is not just purchasing a home. They are establishing the benchmark for an entire market segment.
