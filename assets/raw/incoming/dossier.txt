THE NAPEAGUE CAMPING CLUB DOSSIER

    Working Draft 1.0

    Prepared for XMRT Solutions, Marketing Partners, Buyer Agents, Journalists, and Prospective Purchasers

    EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

    31 Harbor Road is potentially the first publicly marketed residential offering in the history of Napeague Camping Club.

    The significance of this offering extends beyond the physical structure itself.

    The property represents a rare opportunity to acquire a residence within a historically private waterfront community located in Amagansett, New York, one of the most desirable coastal markets in the United States.

    For decades, ownership transfers within the community have largely occurred through internal relationships, family transfers, referrals, and private transactions.

    As a result, most Hamptons buyers, real estate professionals, investors, and journalists have little understanding of the community or its unique ownership structure.

    This offering provides an opportunity to introduce the broader market to one of the most unusual and potentially misunderstood residential communities in the Hamptons.

    CHAPTER 1

    WHAT IS NAPEAGUE?

    Napeague occupies a unique geographical position between East Hampton and Montauk.

    The area serves as a transition zone between two of the most desirable destinations on Long Island.

    The region is known for:

    Ocean beaches

    Surfing

    Fishing

    Boating

    Wildlife preserves

    Open space

    Natural beauty

    Low-density development

    Unlike many Hamptons communities that have experienced significant luxury redevelopment, portions of Napeague maintain a more authentic coastal character.

    Residents often describe the area as one of the last places where old Hamptons culture still survives.

    CHAPTER 2

    THE INVISIBLE COMMUNITY

    Most Hamptons buyers have never heard of Napeague Camping Club.

    Many local residents know it exists.

    Very few understand how it operates.

    Even fewer understand how ownership transfers occur.

    This lack of visibility has created a hidden market.

    While nearby Hamptons properties frequently appear in public listings, marketing campaigns, luxury magazines, and broker databases, the Camping Club has largely remained outside the traditional real estate ecosystem.

    The result is a community that has maintained a degree of privacy rarely found in modern real estate markets.

    CHAPTER 4

    COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS

    Misconception: “It is just a trailer park.”

    Reality: The community contains a wide range of structures and ownership histories. The value proposition is driven by location, lifestyle, scarcity, and community access.

    Misconception: “You don’t own anything.”

    Reality: The ownership structure should be carefully explained through official governing documents. Oversimplified descriptions frequently create confusion.

    Misconception: “It is a condo.”

    Reality: MLS classification systems often force unique ownership structures into categories that may not fully describe the property.

    Misconception: “It is impossible to sell.”

    Reality: The lack of prior public marketing may have limited exposure rather than demand.
