What Makes Napeague / Lazy Point Special
The biggest discovery is that Lazy Point and Napeague are already known among locals as one of the last authentic working-waterfront communities in the Hamptons. Multiple sources describe it as a place that escaped the luxury-development wave that transformed much of the Hamptons.

One article described Lazy Point as:

"like a piece of Nova Scotia tucked in a back corner of Napeague"

That's exactly the type of language that resonates with buyers looking for authenticity rather than another mansion.

Historical Roots
Lazy Point's history goes back to the 1800s when it housed workers associated with the menhaden fishing industry and the Smith Meal Fish Factory, one of the major economic engines of the East End.

The area evolved into a community of:

Baymen
Fishermen
Seasonal cottages
Working waterfront families
rather than the hedge-fund and celebrity culture many people associate with the Hamptons today.

That distinction is powerful.

Napeague Camping Club History
One of the most important facts I found:

The Napeague Camping Club appears to have roots going back to a campground established around 1949. A historical archive from the Montauk Library notes that an original tent-and-trailer site opened in 1949 and later evolved into the Napeague Camping Club.

That gives you a historical timeline:

1949 campground established
Later evolves into Napeague Camping Club
Club incorporated as a New York not-for-profit corporation in 1964
This is exactly the kind of information journalists love.

The "Hidden Hamptons" Narrative Is Real
This isn't marketing fluff.

Napeague itself has a population of only a few hundred people and sits on a narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and Gardiners Bay.

It is literally one of the least populated and least developed parts of the Hamptons.

The area contains:

Napeague Harbor
Lazy Point
The Art Barge
Wildlife preserves
State parks
Fishing grounds
Kiteboarding and windsurfing destinations
