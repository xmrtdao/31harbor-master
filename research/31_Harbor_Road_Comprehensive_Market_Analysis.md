## 4. NAPEAGUE CAMPING CLUB — HISTORY & OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE

### Historical Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1800s | Lazy Point established as housing for Smith Meal Fish Factory workers (menhaden processing) |
| 1938 | Great Hurricane floods entire Napeague strip |
| 1949 | Original tent-and-trailer campground established on the site |
| 1964 | Napeague Camping Club incorporated as New York not-for-profit corporation |
| 1969 | Smith Meal Fish Factory closes due to menhaden overfishing |
| 2019 | East Hampton Town Trustees vote to extend Lazy Point land leases to 35-year terms |
| 2026 | 31 Harbor Road — first public offering in club history |

### Ownership Structure

The Napeague Camping Club operates as a **New York not-for-profit corporation** (incorporated 1964). Key structural elements:

- **Land ownership:** The land beneath Lazy Point homes is owned by the East Hampton Town Trustees (public trust)
- **Lease structure:** Homeowners lease their land from the Trustees — since April 2019, qualified homeowners receive 35-year renewable leases (previously year-to-year)
- **Home ownership:** Residents own the structures themselves
- **Club membership:** The not-for-profit corporation governs the community
- **Transferability:** Historically, ownership transfers occurred through private networks, family relationships, and internal referrals

### Community Character

- **~50 homes** in Lazy Point, privately owned
- **Population:** A few hundred year-round and seasonal residents
- **Demographics:** Mix of original working-waterfront families, baymen, fishermen, and newer seasonal owners
- **Described as:** "Like a piece of Nova Scotia tucked in a back corner of Napeague"
- **Culture:** Last authentic working-waterfront community in the Hamptons — not hedge-fund mansions

### Key Distinctions from Conventional Hamptons Real Estate

| Aspect | Napeague Camping Club | Typical Hamptons |
|--------|----------------------|------------------|
| Ownership | Own structure, lease land | Own land + structure |
| Governance | Not-for-profit corporation | HOA or none |
| Market visibility | Historically private | Publicly marketed |
